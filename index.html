About work of Dr.Norman

Dr. Norman C. Francis is a highly respected figure in education and civil rights. He served as the President of Xavier University of Louisiana for nearly five decades, making significant contributions to higher education, particularly for African American students. Under his leadership, Xavier became a prominent institution known for its academic excellence and commitment to social justice1. Dr. Francis was also involved in various community and national initiatives, including aiding in the recovery efforts after Hurricane Katrina and advising multiple U.S. presidents. His legacy is one of leadership, dedication, and a lifelong commitment to education and equality1.

Timeline of Dr. Norman Borlaug

  • March 25, 1914: Born in Saude, Iowa, USA.
  • 1937: Graduated with a B.S. in Forestry from the University of Minnesota.
  • 1942: Earned a Ph.D. in Plant Pathology and Genetics from the University of Minnesota.
  • 1944: Joined the Rockefeller Foundation's Cooperative Mexican Agricultural Program in Mexico.
  • 1959: Developed high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties in Mexico.
  • 1963: Mexico became a net exporter of wheat due to Borlaug's innovations.
  • 1965-1970: Led the introduction of high-yielding wheat varieties in Pakistan and India, significantly increasing food production.
  • 1970: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to increasing food supply and promoting world peace.
  • 1977: Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from President Jimmy Carter.
  • 1986: Joined Texas A&M University as a Distinguished Professor of International Agriculture.
  • 2006: Awarded the Congressional Gold Medal.
  • September 12, 2009: Passed away in Dallas, Texas, USA.
